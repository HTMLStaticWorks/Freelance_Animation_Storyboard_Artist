MASTERING HIGH-OCTANE ACTION SEQUENCES

Action sequences are the heartbeat of animation. But how do you capture that energy on a static page? The secret lies in understanding the 'Line of Action' and the 'Pacing' of your frames.

1. The Line of Action

Every character in motion should have a clear, simple line that defines their movement. This helps the viewer's eye follow the action effortlessly, even at high speeds.

"Clarity is the first casualty of speed. Your job is to protect it."

2. Varied Framing

Don't just use wide shots. Use extreme close-ups for reaction beats and Dutch angles for tension. This variety creates a cinematic experience within the storyboard.
